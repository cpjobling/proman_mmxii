p-2011-008 – Noise suppresion in physiological signals

Set by Dr Pavel Loskot ( p.loskot@swansea.ac.uk ), Multidisciplinary Nanotechnology Centre ( MNC ).

Available for selection in next round? No

Description

Various types of physiological signals can be collected to determine the human object functioning and performance. Such measurements are often distorted by measurement noises which negatively affects interpretation and evaluation of these signals. In this project, the student will consider digital signal pre-processing techniques that can be used to suppress the measurement noises for a selected class of physiological signals. The noise suppression pre-processing would be then normally followed by feature extraction and classification algorithms to test various hypothesis about the human object under consideration. These post-processing algorithms can be also considered in the project depending on the student's interest.

Special requirements

No special requirements

p-2011-050 – The effect of boot mass on a soccer kick

Set by Dr Iwan Griffiths ( i.w.griffiths@swansea.ac.uk ), Sport and Exercise Science ( SES ).

Available for selection in next round? No

This project has been allocated to student with id: 622043

Description

Soccer kick: boot mass

A simple model of kicking suggests that a heavier boot should produce a greater ball velocity. The student will use 100 Hz 2-D video to measure kicking with soccer boot and with attached masses of 200g, 400g, 600g and 800g. However, the ball velocity tends to remain constant because the extra mass produces a counteracting decrease in foot velocity. The student may need to look at the kicking mechanics to identify the mechanism for the decrease in foot velocity.

p-2011-124 – Why is the current Welsh rugby union team a bunch of losers?

Set by Dr Nick Croft ( t.n.croft@swansea.ac.uk ), Civil and Computational Engineering Centre ( C2EC ).

Available for selection in next round? No

This project has been allocated to student with id: 556013

Description

Over the last 12 months the Welsh team has competed against many of the best teams in the world and lost. Whilst this is not necessarily a bad achievement the fact that in many of these matches the Welsh team had chances to achieve better results and let them slip is of slight concern. A more worrying trend is the performances against the weaker teams such as Italy and Fiji. These are teams that should have been easily beaten given the abilities shown against New Zealand and South Africa. In all matches the Welsh team seem to have a lack of belief in their ability to win the match and performed at a level that was close to that of the opposition. This is in stark contrast to the recent grand slam team which seemed to have a belief that far outstripped the team’s overall skill set.

The underlying question of this project is how do you change the mindset of a team, rather than an individual, in order to stop them underperforming? As a sceptic about the ability of an external factor being able to affect a team’s performance can you scientifically demonstrate the benefit of your ideas to me?

p-2011-147 – Carbon Nanotubes in Sports Equipments

Set by Dr Yon Ju-Nam ( y.k.ju-nam@swansea.ac.uk ), Multidisciplinary Nanotechnology Centre ( MNC ).

Available for selection in next round? No

Description

Nanotechnology is being applied to many areas of sport, especially in the finding of novel materials to obtain better sport equipment performances. Carbon nanotubes have been incorporated in sport equipments for the past few years, particularly by companies such as Yonex, makers of badminton racquets. The remarkable mechanical properties exhibited by carbon nanotubes have simulated much interest in their use to reinforce advanced composites. p-2011-428 – Comparisons between BIA and urine osmolality in measuring exercise induced dehydration in Male athletes.

p-2011-434 – Positioning Techniques for Players

Set by Prof Jinho Choi ( j.choi@swansea.ac.uk ), Multidisciplinary Nanotechnology Centre ( MNC ).

Available for selection in next round? Yes

Description

In order to understand a player’s behaviour in a sport game, it would be essential to track player’s movement and analyze it. This requires positioning techniques for players. While there are various approaches and techniques for positioning, there are different and strict requirement for player’s positioning. As players should not have any impact on their performance from signalling devices that allow movement tracking, the weight of signalling devices should be light. This creates a number of challenging issues. In this project, the review of existing positioning techniques should be carried out first. Among existing techniques, a few candidates should be selected according to the requirements. Computer simulations should be built to understand their limitations and effectiveness in tracking player’s movement.
