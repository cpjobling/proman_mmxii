p-2011-083 – A comparison of Standard and Growing Hierarchical Self-Organizing Maps (SOM) for the analysis of sports-related data.

Set by Prof Stephen Brown ( s.g.r.brown@swansea.ac.uk ), Materials Research Centre ( MRC ).

Description

Following on from three successful projects last year using a Standard SOM approach this project will investigate the potential improvements of using Growing Hierarchical SOMs. Ideally, a pre-existing dataset with many individuals (>50) each with data containing several variables (>10) will be identified and obtained by the student. Such data may be performance-related or health-related.

p-2011-088 – Exercise monitoring: is there an App for that?

Set by Dr Christopher Jobling ( c.p.jobling@swansea.ac.uk ), Multidisciplinary Nanotechnology Centre ( MNC ).

Description

Modern smart phones and high-end mobile devices come with several sensors pre-installed and apps are available that can exploit these sensors to help people gain useful effects from exercise. This project will survey the hardware available and the applications possible with the installed sensors, examine the assumptions behind some of the more popular exercise-based apps and compare these with models from the exercise literature. If you have sufficient interest, the project could be extended towards the development of a suitable exercise monitoring application of your own.

Cross disciplinary research theme(s): Health and Sport

Special requirements

It would be useful if the student has his/her own smart mobile device.

p-2011-147 – Carbon Nanotubes in Sports Equipments

Set by Dr Yon Ju-Nam ( y.k.ju-nam@swansea.ac.uk ), Multidisciplinary Nanotechnology Centre ( MNC ).

Description

Nanotechnology is being applied to many areas of sport, especially in the finding of novel materials to obtain better sport equipment performances. Carbon nanotubes have been incorporated in sport equipments for the past few years, particularly by companies such as Yonex, makers of badminton racquets. The remarkable mechanical properties exhibited by carbon nanotubes have simulated much interest in their use to reinforce advanced composites. Their elastic modules is over 1TPa and tensile strength is over 150 GPa which makes them many times stiffer and stronger than steel while being three to five times lighter. In promoting their products, several manufacturers of sports equipment have advertised carbon nanotubes as reinforcement of some of their top of the line products. The student will carry out literature review in this specific topic and investigate the different challenges of using carbon nanotubes in sports equipment development and manufacturing, and also what needs to be overcome before the carbon nanotubes can be widely used.

p-2011-148 – Anti-doping in Sport: Incongruence in doping in the context of discordant behavioural data – in which measure do we trust?

Set by Dr Yon Ju-Nam ( y.k.ju-nam@swansea.ac.uk ), Multidisciplinary Nanotechnology Centre ( MNC ).

Description

Social psychology research on doping and outcome based evaluation of primary anti-doping prevention and intervention programmes have been dominated by self-reports. Having confidence in the validity and reliability of such data is vital. The student will be required to investigate how both self-reports on behaviour and social cognitive measures could be affected by some form of response bias.
