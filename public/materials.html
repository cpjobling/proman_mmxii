p-2011-070 – Study of a bronze sickle artefact

Set by Prof Valerie Randle ( v.randle@swansea.ac.uk ), Materials Research Centre ( MRC ).

Description

Specimens are available from an ancient bronze sickle. Electron backscatter diffraction and other scanning electron microscope techniques will be used to characterise the microstructure of the parent phase and second phases, notably copper sulphide. Microhardness testing will also be used alongside the microstructure determination, to gain a view of how the sickle may have been made. Some samples may also be heat treated to examine the effect on the microstructure.

Special requirements

Electron Backscatter Diffraction, SEM, furnace, metallographic specimen preparation, optical microscope.

p-2011-080 – Lattice-Boltzmann simulation of flow through a permeable medium.

Set by Prof Stephen Brown ( s.g.r.brown@swansea.ac.uk ), Materials Research Centre ( MRC ).

Description

An existing 2D/3D MATLAB Lattice-Boltzmann simulation of flow through a permeable medium will be assessed with respect to i) liquid flow through micro-scale particles and, time permitting, ii) gas flow through blast furnace burdens. This project will require either MATLAB or FORTRAN90 coding.

p-2011-353 – Investigation of Antarctic Peninsula Ice Sheet via X-Ray Computed Tomography (CT)

Set by Dr Richard Johnston ( r.johnston@swansea.ac.uk ), Materials Research Centre ( MRC ).

Description

Materials science has applications to numerous and diverse fields of research. X-ray computed tomography (CT) is a bridging technology to many of these areas of research. CT can be used to investigate internal structures non-destructively; an extremely valuable tool when it's critical that a specimen remains intact.

This research forms part of the first collaborative project between the Materials Research Centre and the Glaciology group at Swansea. The project will utilise offshore sediment cores recovered from the seabed around the Antarctic Ice Sheet. CT analysis of these samples will allow investigation of deformational structures such as fractures within the sediment body along with porosity and grain density and orientation.

The stability of the marine West Antarctic Ice Sheet is of considerable interest to scientists and policy-makers, on account of climate change and potential for fluctuation in global sea level resulting from changes in ice sheet volume.

Special requirements

Scanning electron microscopy possibly X-ray tomography Mounting, grinding, polishing consumables

p-2011-366 – Atomistic simulation of fracture of copper symmetric tilt grain boundary

Set by Dr Rajib Chowdhury ( r.chowdhury@swansea.ac.uk ), Materials Research Centre ( MRC ).

Description

This project aims to perform an atomistic simulation of fracture of copper symmetric tilt grain boundary. A parallel molecular dynamics code, LAMMPS, will be used to calculate stresses at the grain boundary as the strain of the bicrystal is incrementally increased. Matlab will be used to plot a stress-strain curve, and Visual Molecular Dynamics (VMD) will be used to visualize the simulation.

This molecular dynamics simulation calculates the stress-strain relationship of copper symmetric tilt grain boundary under fracture. The grain boundary structure in this case is a <100> Σ5(210) symmetric tilt grain boundary.. The simulation cell is defined such that the bicrystal is pulled in the y-direction, or perpendicular to the boundary interface, to increase strain. The strain in increased for a specified number of times in a loop, and the stress is calculated at each point before the simulation loops.

Special requirements

LAMMPS, Matlab, VMD
