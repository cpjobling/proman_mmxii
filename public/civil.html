p-2011-207 – Micro-fluid dynamics simulation: CFD modelling of micro-cavity liquid transfer

Set by Dr Davide Deganello ( d.deganello@swansea.ac.uk ), Multidisciplinary Nanotechnology Centre ( MNC ).

Description

Liquid transfer from micro-cavities onto a substrate is at the base of several printing processes. The optimisation of this transfer process is crucial for novel applications such as organic electronic, biological printing. The aim of the project is to model the effect of the cavity shape on the liquid transfer process, using an existing numerical simulation model. The student will be provided of the required background information and of the starting numerical model. The numerical side of the project will be integrated with some basic laboratory experiments to provide the student with a more comprehensive understanding of the modelled physical process.

p-2011-319 – Optimisation of Fourier transform mechanical spectroscopy of biopolymer gelation

Set by Prof Rhodri Williams ( p.r.williams@swansea.ac.uk ), Multidisciplinary Nanotechnology Centre ( MNC ).

Description

The project will seek to establish the optimum frequency content for accurate detemination of the gel point of collagen solutions by Fourier transform mechanical spectroscopy (FTMS) based on small amplitude oscillatory shear. The appropriate level of strain amplitude associated with each frequency component of the FTMS waveform will be independently assessed in both controlled displacement and controlled torque instruments. The project will also seek to establish the limits of collagen concentration, within the physiologically relevant range, for which viable rheometric protocols can be developed.

p-2011-337 – : Effect of Initial Axial Stress on the Vibration of Zinc Oxide Nanotubes

Set by Dr Chengyuan Wang ( chengyuan.wang@swansea.ac.uk ), Civil and Computational Engineering Centre ( C2EC ).

Description

Zinc oxide (ZnO) nanotubes are newly fabricated nanomaterials which exhibit exceptional mechanical properties and unique piezoelectric properties. They are promising for a wide range of applications in nanotechnology, e.g., nanosensors, electroemchanical nano-resonators, electron emission devices and reinforcing fibers in super-strong nanocomposites. The vibration of ZnO nanotubes is one of the major concerns in nanomechanics and the design of ZnO nanotube-based NanoMechancialElectrical Systems. Particularly, the nanotubes usually oscillate under initial stresses generated by external gas pressure, mismatch between different materials, or initial external loadings. Such initial stresses will reduce the structural stiffness of the nanotubes and thus significantly change their vibration frequency and associated deformation patterns. As a result, the study of ZnO nanotube vibration with initial stress is of engineering and scientific interest. In this project, the costâ€“efficient continuum mechanics models will be used to describe the general vibrational behavior of ZnO nanotubes under axial force. The dependence of vibration frequency and associated deformation patterns on the initial stresses will be quantified for various vibration modes by considering the initial stress-induced decrease of the structural stiffness. The outcomes of this project will bring in an in-depth understanding of the effect of initial axial force on ZnO nanotube vibration and provide useful guidance for the design of ZnO nanotube-based nanodevices and nanoelectronics.
